How to Use Meal Planner Pro

Everything you need to know — from adding recipes to syncing your calendar
Getting Started

📖 Adding Recipes

Three ways to build your recipe library:

  1. From a URL — Click "Import from URL" in the Recipes tab. Paste a link from any recipe website (AllRecipes, Budget Bytes, Food Network, etc.) and it auto-scrapes the ingredients and steps.
  2. Manually — Click "+ Add Recipe" to enter a recipe by hand. Fill in name, servings, ingredients, and steps.
  3. Import JSON — Click "Import Recipes" to load recipe JSON files exported from the CLI tool or other sources.

❤️ Favorites & Blocking

Hover over any recipe card to see action buttons:

  1. ❤️ Favorite — Favorited recipes get a boost in Smart Fill, so they appear more often in your meal plans.
  2. 🚫 Block — Blocked recipes will never show up in auto-fill. Use this for recipes you're tired of or didn't like. Click again (🔓) to unblock.
  3. ✏️ Edit — Modify the recipe name, ingredients, steps, or tags.
  4. 🗑️ Delete — Permanently remove a recipe.

🏷️ Tags & Filtering

Recipes have meal types (breakfast, lunch, dinner) and tags (quick, chicken, healthy, etc.).

Use the filter bar at the top of the Recipes tab to show only recipes matching a tag. Smart Fill uses meal types to put the right recipes in the right slots.

Planning Your Week

🧠 Smart Fill Week

The fastest way to plan. One click fills all 21 meal slots for the week.

  1. Go to Plan & Calendar tab
  2. Click "Smart Fill Week"
  3. The algorithm picks recipes that share ingredients (fewer groceries), avoids recently-used recipes, and fills leftovers automatically when a recipe makes multiple servings.

Favorites get a boost. Blocked recipes are excluded.

💰 Budget Fill Week

Fill the week while staying under a dollar target.

  1. Click "Budget Fill" in the Plan tab
  2. Enter your budget (e.g. $75)
  3. It picks the cheapest recipes that fit, based on Kroger prices

Great for tight weeks! Combine with the Pantry tracker to save even more.

🗳️ Family Voting

Let the family pick next week's meals.

  1. Click "Family Vote" in the Plan tab
  2. Share the link with family members (text, email, etc.)
  3. Everyone swipes through recipes and votes 👍 or 👎
  4. Results show which recipes are most popular

Works on phones — no app install needed!

📅 Manual Planning

Click any meal slot to manually pick a recipe. Switch between Weekly and Calendar views using the tabs. Use the arrow buttons to navigate between weeks/months.

Click "Clear Week" to start over. Individual slots can be cleared by hovering and clicking ✕.

Shopping & Cooking

🛒 Grocery List

Your grocery list auto-generates from the current week's meal plan.

  1. Go to the Grocery List tab
  2. Items are consolidated and categorized by store section
  3. Pick a store (Kroger, Whole Foods, Costco, or Cheapest Mix) to see prices
  4. Use Scale to double (2x) or halve (0.5x) quantities
  5. Check off items as you shop

📱 Send to Phone

Get the grocery list on your phone for shopping:

  1. "Text" — Opens your Messages app with the full list ready to send
  2. "Email" — Opens your email client with a formatted list + meal plan
  3. "Copy" — Copies markdown to clipboard (paste into Notes, Reminders, etc.)

🥫 Pantry Tracker

Track what you already have so you only buy what you need.

  1. Go to the Pantry tab
  2. Add items you have at home (use quick-add buttons for staples)
  3. The Grocery List automatically subtracts pantry items — you'll see "In Pantry" for items fully covered and reduced quantities for partial matches

🍳 What Can I Make Tonight?

Don't know what to cook? Let the app figure it out.

  1. Go to the Tonight? tab
  2. Type in what ingredients you have (comma-separated)
  3. Click "Find Recipes"
  4. See recipes ranked by match % — green items = you have it, red = you'd need to get it
Pricing & Nutrition

💲 Store Pricing

Prices are pre-loaded for Kroger, Whole Foods, and Costco. The Pricing tab lets you:

  1. Edit any price to match your local store
  2. Import real Kroger prices from the CLI tool (python kroger_api.py update)
  3. See which store is cheapest per ingredient
  4. Weekly cost comparison shows at the bottom of the Plan tab

🥗 Nutrition Tracking

Calorie and macro estimates are shown automatically:

  1. Recipe cards show estimated calories
  2. Recipe detail (click a card) shows full breakdown: calories, protein, carbs, fat per recipe and per serving
  3. Estimates are based on common ingredient data — accuracy improves with standard ingredients
Calendar Sync (Skylight, Google, Apple)

📅 Auto-Sync to Your Calendars

Your meal plan is published to a live URL that calendars subscribe to. Set up once, updates automatically!

  1. Google Calendar: Go to calendar.google.com → Click + next to "Other calendars" → "From URL" → Paste: https://tosterl.github.io/Meal-Planner-Grociery-List/meal-plan.ics
  2. Skylight Calendar: Either sync via Google Calendar (Skylight auto-pulls from Google), or in Skylight app → Settings → Calendars → Add → paste the URL above
  3. Apple Calendar: File → New Calendar Subscription → paste the URL
  4. Outlook: Add calendar → From internet → paste the URL

To update the calendar, run python publish.py from the terminal. A GitHub Action also runs every Sunday at 6 PM ET to auto-publish a fresh plan.

Tips & Tricks

💡 Pro Tips

  1. Leftovers save time — Recipes with 4+ servings auto-fill later meals as leftovers. You cook less!
  2. Stock your pantry first — Add common staples (oil, rice, spices) to the Pantry tab. Your grocery list shrinks dramatically.
  3. Use "Cheapest Mix" — In the Grocery List, select "Cheapest Mix" as the store to see the lowest price across all stores for each item.
  4. Scale for crowds — Having guests? Set Scale to 2x in the Grocery List to double all quantities.
  5. Import recipe URLs — Way faster than typing. Works with 500+ recipe sites including AllRecipes, Budget Bytes, Food Network, and more.

🔄 Typical Weekly Workflow

  1. Optional: Share the Family Vote link over the weekend
  2. Sunday: Click Smart Fill Week (or let the GitHub Action do it automatically)
  3. Review the plan, swap out anything you don't feel like
  4. Check the Grocery List — pantry items are already subtracted
  5. Hit "Text" to send the list to your phone
  6. Shop at Kroger (or use python publish.py --kroger --zip YOUR_ZIP for pickup)
  7. Cook and enjoy! Check the calendar on your Skylight for what's on today.
